Case Study 2

Voter Apathy Among College Students

A group composed of some student researchers and myself were tasked with identifying the causes of voter apathy and increasing voter turnout among college students. Starting from the question “How might we increase voter engagement and participation among students?” our team created a formal research plan of a cognitive walkthrough with a post-walkthrough interview and love break up letter, as well as five graffiti walls around our campus, and an expert interview with a local city councilman and high school teacher.

After collecting the various stories, we did a group synthesis coming to two key conclusions:

1) voting is heavily tied to familial relationships
2) Most students are vastly undereducated about the out-of-state voting process.

These conclusions helped us plan a prototype, that we implemented live, of a voter education and empathy program that could be implemented in schools. We intended to make a prototype that was business viable so we used the BookIt! program as a model for voter education.
