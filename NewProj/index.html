Case Study #1

Voter Turnout Among College Students

What We Did: I and another group of student researchers were tasked with identifying the causes of voter apathy and increasing voter turnout among college students. Starting from the question “How might we increase voter engagement and participation among students?” our team created a research plan of personal interview consisting of a cognitive walkthrough of the voting process, a love/break-up letter to the electoral system, and a reflective questionnaire. We also did an expert interview with a local city council member and a graffiti wall in various parts of Southern Methodist University campus.

After collecting the data we did a group synthesis coming to two key conclusions: voting is heavily tied to familial relationships, and most students are vastly undereducated about the out of city voting process. These conclusions helped us plan a prototype of a voter education system that could be implemented in schools using the BookIt! program as a model and planning for a partnership with participatory food chains.

Case Study #2

Dallas Crime

What I Did: I and a few other students were given the information from the Dallas Police Department for 2014-2015. We were tasked with using various data mining strategies to create a report in the style of one that could be given to the Dallas Police. I used various techniques, such as classification, association, and clustering to find trends and patterns in the data.

With these insights we presented the report with a full business understanding of the implications and time that would be required to change the current protocol. It was important to us that we know the deeper kernels of insight in our data, and present them with the best way to use them, and how to support their validity with user/field research.
